Hurricane Katrina (MS and AL General)

MS/AL
Subject Wrack line with debris, Grand Bay, Alabama
Posting Date 2005-Sep-26 
Hurricane Katrina washed debris across hundreds of meters of coastal 
marsh, creating big "wrack" lines far from the water.  In the marshes 
of Alabama's Grand Bay, this debris line is also far from roads.  Here, 
the outer marshes have been swept clean and tons and tons of woody 
debris, household waste, chemical drums, and damaged vessels have been
deposited at the tree line.  The removal of hazardous materials in this 
location begins with marking their locations from the air.  The next 
task is to--safely--walk to the debris line to determine which of the 
tanks are empty and which vessels contain fuel that must be removed.

- Photo and text provided by NOAA Scientific Support Coordinator
