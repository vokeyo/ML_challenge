1. NOI: DCR Beach Management Plan

Christine Player of Vine Associates represented the DCR. Ms. Vine stated that she previously provided the Commission with an updated Beach Management Plan (Plan) and a Plan summary.

 

Rick Whaley stated that wrack plays an integral part in the health of the beach system and said he would like more information/clarification on the cleaning of the beach and handling of the wrack on the beach in the Plan. Jim Marcellos (DCR) stated that the normal process is to spread the wrack out along the beach behind the fence, however, if it is filled with debris they take the wrack back for cleaning but otherwise the wrack is placed behind the fence. He stated the smell issue can be controlled with the use of sand. Mike Magnifico confirmed that putting the wrack behind the fence is a new practice. Rick Whaley stated he supports the DCR trying to give back to the dune.

 

Rick Whaley asked if there could be a plan to replace dune crossings, perhaps one or two a year, instead of only when they have to be replaced. Ray Foucher said that there are standards for replacing the boardwalks. He mentioned the significant cost which would be incurred to replace a boardwalk. Mr. Foucher said that when the Salisbury Trust is established and when funds are available they will deal with the issue of replacing dune crossings. Rick Whaley advised Mr. Foucher that Ron Laffely had a design for elevated, adjustable dune crossings that he may give to the Commission. Ray Foucher said that if Mr. Laffely gives the design he will evaluate it.

3. NOI: Louis Minicucci, 79 Atlantic Avenue

Sally Laffely and Janelle of Fulcrum Architects represented the applicant. Janelle said the plans have been updated and that in the new proposal there are additional stairs to get further up on the dune. Janelle reviewed the revised plans with the Board. Janelle stated the new proposal is at more of an angle. Iora Alexander asked what the width was and Janelle said the walkway and stairs are three feet wide. In response to a question from Rick Whaley, Jim Marcellos said the plan changes are due to a meeting between the applicant and them. Jim Marcellos said on-grade does not apply on this property. He said this walk-over will now be at the proper angle. Jim Marcellos said the DCR is comfortable with this plan. The Board discussed plans for plantings under the stairs and Janelle said there are no plans for plantings. Janelle said the platform can be elevated as the dune fills in. Jim Marcellos said that usually fencing and planting of dune grass is a good idea but it is not necessary at this property.

 

Iora Alexander moved to Approve the plan for stairs for NOI: Louis Minicucci, 79 Atlantic Avenue as presented. Rick Whaley seconds the motion. The motion was unanimously approved.

5. Amended NOI: Louis Fossarelli, 49 Commonwealth Avenue

Sally Laffely and Janelle of Fulcrum Architects represented the applicant. Janelle said that originally this was presented as a renovation using the existing foundation but that now the applicant will raze the entire structure and rebuild. Janelle stated there will be a wood pile foundation and the same floor plan. Janelle said it will be three foot six inches off grade to meet the minimum base flood elevation. Janelle said there will be geotextile fabric and stone dust and gravel as allowed by the DEP. Iora Alexander questioned if originally there was going to be a garage and Janelle said that instead of having a wide open garage, that area will now be storage and mechanical space. Iora Alexander questioned where the mechanical room would be and Janelle said the mechanical space will be in the structure but that there will be a man hole which will be enclosed. In response to a question, Janelle said the enclosed area will be five feet in diameter. Larry O達rien questioned the beams and said that as proposed these beams cannot be treated. Mr. O達rien advised Janelle to check the details to see if they need to use another product.

 

Iora Alexander stated that the previous applicant was denied a geotextile membrane. Sheila Albertelli said she wants the Board to be consistent will all homeowners and she said she is surprised that the DEP is allowing an exception for stone dust. Rick Whaley read into the record the September 15, 2008 e-mail from Eric Worrall, DEP, to Nancy White, DEP, allowing an exception for Mr. Fossarelli to place a geotextile membrane with a gravel/stone dust mixture for a parking area. Rick Whaley asked what were the significant compromises and Sally Laffely said the significant costs. Rick Whaley mentioned allowing the entire project except for the geotextile membrane and stone dust. Sheila Albertelli said the Board has to be consistent to all and thinks the exception is detrimental to the beach. The Agent stated she spoke to the DEP regarding the fabric and stone dust and she stated this is unfair. Sheila Albertelli agreed saying this was unfair to the environment and other applicants. Sally Laffely asked if the Commission could recommend alternatives. The Board and Sally Laffely discussed that there could be a continuance, an approval for the project without allowing the stone dust and fabric, or a denial. The Agent said allowing the applicant to do this through an amendment was a huge exception. Sally Laffely recommended a continuance allowing Mr. Fossarelli to speak with Ron Laffely and the DEP.

 

Iora Alexander moved to Continue Amended NOI: Louis Fossarelli, 49 Commonwealth Avenue to October 15, 2008 at 7:10 p.m. so the owner can speak to Ron Laffely. Larry O達rien seconds the motion. The motion carried with Sheila Albertelli, Larry O達rien, and Iora Alexander voting in the affirmative. Rick Whaley voted against the motion.
