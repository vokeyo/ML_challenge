Virginia Civil War Battlefields Wetland Classification Digital Maps
Entry ID: usgs_nwrc_virginiabattlefields

  [ View Brief Record ] [ Update this Record ]
Summary
This data set consists of wetlands delineations for the Virginia Civil War
battlefields (Fredericksburg-Spotsylvania National Military Park). The purpose
of the dataset is to conduct wetlands mapping to represent ecological and
biological data derived from current aerial photography.

Aerial photography was acquired for the project area. Following a ground
... truthing exercise, the photos were then interpreted according to their wetland
habitats; a Bausch Lomb stereoscope was using for the photointerpretation
process at a 10x magnification. The delineated data was then transferred to
base maps using a Zoom Transfer Scope; this process removes any distortion from
the image. In all of these processes, the product is throughly scrutinized
using standard operating procedures for quality control and assessment. The
mylar overlays were then digitized using an Altek Datatab digitizer. Four
control points corresponding to the four corners of the quadrangle were used
for registration during data collection. Cover type features continuing onto
adjacent quadrangles were adjusted to intersect the quad boundary at a common,
coincident node. Attributes were assigned during either the digitizing or
editing stages.

This metadata was originally created in Biological Data Profile format by
Helena Schaeffer of the U.S. Geological Survey, National Wetlands Research
Center.

Quality
Interpreted photos were quality checked by two photointerpreters for attribute
classification. Field verification was performed for chosen areas of the study.
The mylar based maps were checked by cartographic personnel for line and
labelworks, following mapping conventions and standards. The digital attribute
accuracy is tested by manually comparing hard copy plots ... of the data with
source materials. When attributes cannot be visually verified on plots, they
are interactively queried and verified on screen. In addition, the attributes
are compared against a master set of valid attributes.

ARC/INFO software is used to create and maintain topological relationships
between features. There are no duplicate features. Polygonal features begin and
end at the same point, contain no overshoots or undershoots, and contain one
label. Linear features are continuous where appropriate,i.e., dangling arcs are
removed if they are not required.

For cartographic process, accordance with nationally accepted mapping standards
and conventions were followed. Processing of digital data follows conventions
and standard methodologies for the time period and within limitations of
available workstation equipment at the date of data development.
