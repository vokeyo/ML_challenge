The problem with the correction shown is that it raises the IP at CDF which is already high by 267 microns.

Below is a compromise solution that does not raise the collision point at CDF.  This is essentially what we agreed to do in September 2003 to keep the collision point below the Tevatron ideal elevation.

Something worth noting here is the correction applied by VA47.  Before the shutdown, VA47 was running at .033 mrad at flattop, and at .059 mrad at collisions.  For both the desired and the compromised solutions, VA47 was set to -.033 mrad at flattop, and to zero at collisions.  This essentially changes the initial condition of the beam coming into the region in this analysis.  If the changes made to VA47 are taken at face value, it would run at zero at flattop after the quad moves, and at .059 mrad at collisions (the same as it did before the shutdown).
