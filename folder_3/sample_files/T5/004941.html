OPEN SPACE PRESENTS:

“Last One Out,

Turn Off the Lights,

or

How I Learned To Stop Worrying and Love Coyote”

~works by Jaune Quick-to-See Smith

What: Painting and Drawing Show Exploring the Character of Coyote in Southwest Culture and Demonstrations

Where: Open Space Visitor Center

6500 Coors Blvd., NW

(Between Montaño & Paseo del Norte at Bosque Meadows Rd.)

505-897-8831

When: Opening Saturday March 7, 2009 1pm – 4pm, Show runs through April 28, 2009

Admission: Free

Contact: Joshua Willis, Parks & Recreation Dept., Open Space Division, 505-897-8831, jrwillis@cabq.gov

 

 

Jaune Quick-to-See Smith

Jaune Quick-to-See Smith's show at the Open Space Visitor Center will consider the place Coyote holds: both in the stories of many Native American peoples, as a mischievous caretaker and bringer of light and wisdom, and as a member of the everyday ecology of nearly the entire North American continent.

Opening and Demonstrations

Opening:  Saturday March 7, 2009 1pm – 4pm Come and meet the artists!  There will be a monotype demonstration by Judy Just and reception snacks in the kitchen.

Coyote Talk:  March 14, 10 am – 11:30 am Mike Bochnia’s personal experience as a tracker has led him to an appreciation for all creatures including coyote.  Mike will speak about the coyote’s role in the web of life.  He will consider both coyote’s dietary needs as an omnivore, and how coyote plays into the cultural heritage of the Southwest. 

Monotype Demonstration: Judy Just will also host a class demonstrating the art of Monotype, April 18 from 1pm – 4pm at the Open Space Visitor Center. Limit 12 people.  “Have you ever wanted to try printmaking but thought you needed a press?  In this workshop we will explore additive, reductive and trace monotype without a press.  With cutting edge intaglio inks it is easy, fun and safe to make wonderful monotypes using beautiful layers of color. Come and enjoy the fun.  For more info about making monotypes go to wbinks.com and judyjust.com. ”

Judy Just is an experienced artist and instructor with many years experience teaching adults and children, in various media. She received her BFA from Washington University in St. Louis with continuing studies at Ringling School of Art, Instituto Allende, Mexico and privately in Greece. 

About the Artist and Coyote

Increasingly, Coyote is being driven from his home, hunted down, even exterminated. The cost of such blindfolded imprudence directly impairs the health of our agriculture, as rodent and pest populations are allowed to go unchecked in the absence of a normalizing predator. Even the health of our families comes under risk, as diseases that would be controlled by coyote along with those rodent and pest populations, seep into our very living spaces.

So he's a thief, maybe a scoundrel? But how can we stay here, when our Prairies and Mesas go haywire? Will we hide behind airtight walls and poison? We need Coyote. When the mice and rabbits ruin our crops and squirrels strip the trees, will Coyote come back? And if Coyote is gone, and the wolf and the cougar are hungry, where will they turn? Coyote keeps us safe. Bring Coyote back!

Jaune Quick-to-See Smith was born at the Indian Mission on the Flathead Reservation. She is an enrolled Flathead Salish member of the Confederated Salish and Kootenai Tribes of the Flathead Indian Nation, Montana.

She received an Associate of Arts Degree at Olympic College in Bremerton Washington. She attended the University of Washington in Seattle, received her BA in Art Education at Framingham State College and a masters degree in art at the University of New Mexico.

She is in the collections of the Museum of Modern Art, Quito, Ecuador; the Museum of Mankind, Vienna, Austria; The Walker, Minneapolis, MN; Smithsonian American Art Museum, Washington DC; the Museum of Modern Art, the Brooklyn Museum, the Metropolitan, The Whitney Museum, NY and the Victoria and Albert Museum, London.

Smith calls herself a cultural art worker. Elaborating on her Native American worldview, Smith’s work addresses today’s tribal politics, human rights, and environmental issues with a keen sense of humor.

Her favorite quote is, "I want my art to be off-balance, tell stories, be political, humorous, messy, show images and have to do with real life.”

We are proud to host Jaune Quick-to-See Smith at the Open Space Visitor Center through the months of March and April.  To accompany her work we have planned events which will demonstrate the importance of coyote to healthy ecosystem including: a “personalized” look into the importance of coyote, and a printmaking (mono type) demonstration.  Please check our website for more information and times.
