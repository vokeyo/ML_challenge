DAY 1...

...GTLKS...
VERY LIGHT WAA SNOWS WILL BRUCH PORTIONS OF THE NRN AND ERN GT
LAKES AS LOW PRES TRACKS GENLY EWD ALONG THE SRN CANADIAN BORDER.
NO REAL UPR SUPPORT HERE FOR MUCH MORE THAN LIGHT AMOUNTS..WITH
PERHAPS .10 INCH OR SO TO THE NRN SHORES EAST OF LAKE ONTARIO IN
SOMEWHAT MORE FAVORABLE LOW LEVEL FLOW.

..LOWER MS VALLEY...
WEAK AND VERY SHEARED S/WV ENERGY ASSOCD WITH E/W SUBTROP JET WILL
INTERACT WITH INCREASING RETURN FLOW MSTR PRIMARILY LATE IN THE PD
ACRS THE WRN GULF TO SUPPORT AN INCREASE IN RAIN/SHWRS THIS
REGION.  WITH THE GULF STARTING OUT SO VERY DRY..DID NOT GO TOO
HEAVY..BUT DID GO SLIGHTLY ABOVE GFS/NAM AMOUNTS WITH SOME .25
INCH AMOUNTS OVER SRN LA IN MODEST RETURN FLOW AND PWS.

..PAC NW...
S/WV ENERGY LIFTING NEWD ACRS THE ERN GULF OF AK WILL DIRECT MOIST
FRONTAL SYSTEM TOWARD THE B.C. COAST.  85H WLY FLOW INCREASES TO
BETWEEN 30 TO 40 KTS LATE SUN TOWARD THE OLYMPICS WITH PWS APCHG
AN INCH.  BASED ON CURRENT TIMING..EXPECT LIGHT RAINS TO REACH THE
OLYMPICS AND NRN WA CASCADES LATE IN THE PD WITH NAM/GFS AMOUNTS
APPEARING REASONABLE.

...DAYS 2 AND 3...

...GULF COAST/LWR MS AND TN VLYS TO THE EAST COAST...

SRN STREAM ENERGY CURRENTLY MOVING ACROSS NRN BAJA CA IS FORECAST
TO MOVE INTO THE NWRN GOMEX LATE DAY 1/EARLY DAY 2.  DEEP MOISTURE
LIFTING OUT AHEAD OF A DEVELOPING SFC LOW WILL FUEL MDT/HVY PCPN
ALONG THE CTRL/ERN GULF COAST ON DAY 2...WITH A SECONDARY MAX
FORMING ALONG A NRN STREAM BNDRY DRAPED NE-SW FROM THE TN VLY INTO
THE LWR MS VLY.  SFC LOW WILL LIFT NEWD OFF OF THE E COAST ON DAY
3 AS THE SRN STREAM ENERGY LIFTS OUT AHEAD AN AMPLIFYING NRN
STREAM TROF OVER THE GREAT LAKES/OH VLY.  THERE IS STILL A FAIR
AMT OF MODEL UNCERTAINTY WITH THIS SYSTEM...HOWEVER THE MODELS
APPEAR TO BE CONVERGING ON A LESS-PHASED MORE PROGRESSIVE
SYSTEM...WHICH IN TURN MEANS LIGHTER AMTS FOR THE ERN SEABOARD.
THE HPC FORECAST REFLECTS THIS TREND...DISCOUNTING THE HEAVIER
AMTS SHOWN BY THE GFS ALONG THE MID ATL/NORTHEAST...WHICH DESPITE
ITS OWN TRENDS...IS STILL DEEPER THAN THE REMAINING 12Z
OPERATIONAL SOLUTIONS.

...PAC NW/NRN ROCKIES...

LIGHT OROGRAPHICALLY FOCUSED AMTS...MAINLY ACROSS WA/NRN ID AND
NWRN MT ON DAY 2 ARE EXPECTED TO DIMINISH ON DAY 3 AS AN UPR RIDGE
BUILDS AHEAD OF THE NRN HALF OF A SPLITTING TROF OVER THE E PAC.
