Expertise:
Expert in integrated pest management (IPM) for ornamental plants. Educational assistance and research related to control of insect and mite pests, pest monitoring, selection of appropriate control measures and treatment evaluations.

Education:
B.S. Biology, Juniata College, 1989
M.S. Entomology, University of Maryland, 1991

Station career:
Assistant Scientist, Department of Entomology, 1998 - Present

Past research:
Established the first IPM program for the Connecticut nursery industry. Have conducted educational lectures on ornamental pests and IPM to pesticide applicator license trainees, arborist license trainees, grounds keepers and college students; and also entomology lectures for civic organizations, garden clubs and children’s groups. Research has focused on integration of selective miticides and predatory mites for control of twospotted spider mites.

Current research:
Evaluated the effect of mycorrhizal fungi and biostimulants on the survival and health of recently transplanted trees and shrubs with Tom Rathier, CAES Valley Laboratory.  Conducting an economic assessment of IPM adoption at commercial nurseries in Connecticut using the rhododendron leafminer (Lyonetia latistrigella) as a model.
