11/4/2006 - News Release: UPDATE Burglary of a Habitation with intent to commit Sexual Assault

« Back

From:     Master Officer Rhonda Seaton
Date:     November 4, 2006
Subject: Press Release  UPDATE   11/04    1317 HOURS

On 11/01/06, at approximately 0554 hours, the College Station Police Department received a call from a female victim that a black man had entered her bedroom and attempted to sexually assault her.  Within minutes of arriving on scene, officers found that two other attempted sexual assaults had occurred at the same apartment complex around the same time.  All three female victims reside at 305 Marion Pugh Drive (Callaway Villas Apartments).

The victims reported that they were asleep and awoke to find the actor in their bed partially undressed with his penis exposed.  They reported that the male was also attempting to remove portions of their clothing.  It appears that all three incidents occurred within a 45 minute time frame.  When the victims discovered the actor in their bed they began to scream and order the actor to leave.  The actor fled the scene in each instance and none of the victims were injured.  There was no forced entry found in any of the instances and it appears that the actor probably entered the apartments through unlocked front doors and then entered the female’s bedrooms through unlocked bedroom doors.  The case has been turned over to the College Station Police Department Criminal Investigation Division.  This division will be working with the some of the victims, witnesses and a Texas Department of Public Safety artist to develop a sketch of the actor.  This sketch will be released to the public as soon as possible.  Police are asking anyone with information regarding this case to contact the police department at (979) 764-3600.

Actor Description:

Black male    Long (below the knee) denim shorts
5’6” – 5’7”   Dark oversized t-shirt style shirt with white or silver
Medium build   writing on the back directly below the collar of the
Approximately 165 pounds  shirt
Short black hair

Additional information regarding home security tips can be obtained from the College Station Police Department web site at www.cstx.gov/police.  More information will be released as it is obtained.

UPDATE:  The College Station Police Department has just been notified of another incident that occurred this morning at approximately 0400 hours at the same location.  We do not have many details regarding the last reported incident.  However, we now believe this will actually be the first incident.  Additional details will be released as they become available.  Furthermore, we still do not have a clear age range on the actor.
UPDATE:  Further investigation regarding the fourth reported incident has confirmed that the victim (in what we now know was probably the first incident to occur) was actually sexually assaulted.  At this point, this is the only reported incident where the actor was successful in his attempt to sexually assault his victim.  Police are investigating several other reported similar attempted incidents that are said to have occurred in the same complex during the same time frame.  At this time investigators are still following up leads and reports at the complex and a more current number of confirmed incidents will be available in the morning.
UPDATE:  At this time the College Station Police Department has a total of nine reported offenses related to the original incident.  We now know that the actor originally entered the apartment as a guest of a resident.  However, the actor was just an acquaintance of the resident and the resident only knows that the male told her friend that his name was Marcus.  It is not known whether or not this is the person’s real name.  The resident allowed “Marcus” into the apartment and during his time there he attempted to sexually assault her.  This report is classified as “criminal attempt sexual assault” due to the fact that he was invited into the apartment.  The additional “burglary of a habitation with intent to commit sexual assault” report taken was classified as such due to the fact the actor entered this particular apartment without permission and attempted to sexually assault one of the residents.  In the three “criminal trespass” reports, the actor entered apartments, uninvited, but was told to leave by persons inside the apartments.  In these cases, the actor left without incident.  Investigators have also been able to determine the age of the actor to be between 18 and 22 years of age.

The Texas Department of Public Safety forensic artist is currently working with victims and witnesses to develop a sketch of the suspect.  We will release the sketch as soon as it is completed.

UPDATE:  On 11/02/06, an officer with the College Station Police Department advised that upon seeing the sketch of the suspect they recognized him to be a person known to them as Marcus Anthony Wilson.  On 11/03/06, after viewing a photograph line-up, one of the victims positively identified Wilson as the person that was unlawfully in his apartment.  Based on this positive identification, CSPD detectives were able to obtain a warrant for Wilson’s arrest for the offense of “criminal trespass in a habitation.”  On 11/04/06 at approximately 0107 hours, CSPD officers working in the Northgate area located Wilson inside a bar in that area.  He was positively identified and placed under arrest for the warrant.  Another officer transported Wilson to CSPD for booking.  After Wilson was transported from the area a female approached the arresting officers and asked if the arrest that they had just made was related to the Callaway Villas incident.  The female further stated that the person that had just been arrested was the same person that she had found in her bedroom on 11/01/06.  Prior to the female approaching the officers with information neither of the officers knew her nor had they had any interaction with her.  Detectives with CSPD were able to obtain warrants for six counts of “burglary of a habitation with intent to commit sexual assault.”  An original charge for “sexual assault” was changed to “burglary of a habitation with intent to commit sexual assault” because the latter charge is a higher offense.  Bond has been set at $150,000 for each offense for a total bond of $900,000.

Burglary of a habitation with intent to commit sexual assault is a first degree felony punishable by life imprisonment or for any term of not more than 99 years or less than 5 years and a fine not to exceed $10,000.
