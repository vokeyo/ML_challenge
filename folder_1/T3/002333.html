straps or chains on the gorrilla would be a choice I would make on the intent of the installation. If your hanging "club bowstands" that are going to stay for a long period of time I would definately rig a chain and plan on refitting the stand to the tree annually so it doesnt grow in. If its a short term installation I dont see a safety issue at all with the straps. I do check those straps regularly for dry rot, squirrell chews ect.

Again, though, if your looking for a personal stand thats going in and out of the woods, Conz is right on the Lone Wolf set up is the smart bowhunters choice. It is by far the most packable, quiet and easiest to install bowhunting loc on I have used.The Lone wolf sticks are simply awesome IMO. You can climb crooked trees with ease and they install silently. Id buy six sticks, one alpha loc on, and two attachment chains. You hunt with four always leaving the two top sticks and loc on chain . This keeps your gear safe from most theives and your pre hunt installation even simpler than starting from scratch.
