A.  PUBLIC HEARING at 6:00pm:

 

1. NOI:  DCR, 218 Beach Rd. - Beach Management Plan

 

Mr. Silva, Susan Hamilton, Melissa Martini, and Mike Magnifico appeared for the DCR with their consultant, Christine Player.   Ms. Player continued the review of the Salisbury Barrier Beach Management Plan (Plan) Recommendations.   Comments included:

  • Thomas Hughes wants the Plan to have a process to ensure sure the wrack material is cleaned.  He is concerned that  fishing lines, debris, and bottles may be left in the wrack.  Ms. Player said they will provide more detail in the Plan.
  • Thomas Hughes requested that the Plan have a formalized process to keep a record/tracking of animal burials especially for mammals (whales).
  • Thomas Hughes requested that the Plan include a process to monitor the invasive species.
  • Chuck Takesian asked that the Plan contain provisions for private homeowners to bring sand onto the reservation in order to save their homes.
  • Thomas Hughes said the DCR should work with local Town groups when deciding when to release sand.  He recommended more input and coordination with the Town on the stockpiling and releasing of sand.
  • Thomas Hughes said that during emergency conditions that there should be a process for the DCR to place sand while simultaneously applying for an Emergency Certification.
  • Thomas Hughes wants the Plan to have provisions allowing the DCR to block unsafe access areas.  
  • Thomas Hughes said the Plan should encourage people to share private access ways to the beach.

  • Thomas Hughes said there should be a notification form/permitting process for  temporary beach events, e.g., Sand and Sea.  The notification process would still provide the Town  review and oversight of the events.

3.  NOI:  David Wolpert 60-62 Lafayette Road

 

David Wolpert and Michael Wolpert appeared before the Board.  Michael Wolpert said the applicant wants to construct a garage attached to the existing house and stated there will be a dry well.  He stated the applicant received approval from the Zoning Board.  Sheila Albertelli asked if the Wetland line went out for review and Michael Wolpert said it was done previously.  Thomas Hughes questioned where the water from the garage would go, the stormwater calculations, and the change in pervious surface.  Michael Wolpert said these calculations were not done. Thomas Hughes said this should be done for the Board to review.  Agent Rowden advised the applicant that the DEP has a new stormwater checklist since last January.

 

Abutter – An abutter mentioned sewer line concerns, wanted confirmation that the garage would only be used for what the applicant said, a concern for taking down trees, and concerns for flooding and erosion.  David Wolpert reviewed the Plans with the Board and the abutter.  Iora Alexander questioned utilities and Mike Wolpert said they will run off the existing lines.  He stated they have to go before the Planning Board.  Thomas Hughes questioned the stairs, he asked what the setback was, and he asked if they would be open underneath.  Mike Wolpert said they would be open.

 

Thomas Hughes moved to Continue  NOI:  60-62 Lafayette Road  to July 16, 2008 at 7:10 p.m. and  that in the interim the Applicant is to:

  • Provide the calculations for the stormwater
  • Have Engineering Review of the stormwater by the Town’s consultant
  • Have the Wetland line reviewed by Mary Rimmer.

Iora Alexander seconds the motion.  In discussion, Agent Rowden explained the need for an escrow account for the reviews, said she would provide estimates, and advised the Applicant he would need to provide the check to pay for the reviews.  The motion was unanimously approved.
