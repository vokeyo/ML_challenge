Topic: COUCILMEMBERS TO JOIN LOBBY EFFORT IN WASHINGTON
Category(s): City Council
Release Date: 03/07/08

Several Councilmembers will be participating in the National League of Cities' (NLC) Congressional City Conference in Washington, D.C. from March 8-12. This meeting of municipal leaders is one of Rochester's best chances to help make the needs of our City heard in the nation's capital.

Congress will be considering several key issues that directly impact our cities, including crime reduction, home ownership financing, and education reform. Policy discussions and legislative briefings have been scheduled, and conference delegates will meet with Congressional and Administration leaders to talk about urban priorities.

NLC, the oldest and largest municipal organization in the United States, is a bi-partisan national research and advocacy organization, and has been responsible for millions of federal dollars being spent in American cities. Rochester has been active in NLC for more than fifty years.

For more information, contact Bridget Monroe at  585-428-6175.
