Manual Appendix 3-7-F

RECOMMENDED CORE REFERENCES

 

The drug -information library of pharmacies at all facilities shall be adequate for the, needs of the facility as determined by ' the scope and size of the facility. At least one reference from each category shall be available. References preceded by an asterisk (*) must available at the facility. Only the most current edition of major references shall be maintained.

Reference monographs more than two years old and textbooks more, than five years old are of very limited value. Each pharmacy service shall include drug information references in the annual budget request.

JOURNALS

A drug information library shall include appropriate journals. Normally these include one or more general medical journals, selected clinical pharmacy journals and other journals which meet the information needs of the facility. Journals commonly cited to be included are the following:

  • American Journal of Hospital Pharmacy
  • American Journal of Medicine
  • Annals of Internal Medicine
  • Clinical Pharmacy
  • Drug Intelligence and Clinical Pharmacy
  • Journal of the American Medical Association
  • New England Journal of Medicine

The pharmacy service shall have available the current Recommendations of the Advisory Committee on Immunizations of the Centers for Disease Control as published in Morbidity and Mortality Weekly Reports (MMWRI. The lead articles from MMWR (including immunization recommendations) are published weekly in the Journal of the American Medical Association.
