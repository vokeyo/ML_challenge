Advanced Wetland Photo-Interpretation Workshop
When:November 1, 2004
Description:This three day workshop will cover: The development of a methodology for mapping wetlands projects, an understanding of the NWI wetland and the National Wetlands Research Center upland classification systems, Photo-interpretation of color infrared aerial photographs, the use of soil surveys, topographic maps and historical wetland maps as aids in the Photo-interpretation process, techniques for groundtruthing, and cartographic transformation of photo-interpreted habitats to a standard base map. This course will include classroom lecture, classroom exercises in photo-interpretation of habitats using a stereoscope, fieldwork, cartographic transformation using a zoom transfer scope. An understanding of the identification of wetland habitats from aerial photographs (*as presented in the Introduction to Remote Sensing and Photo-interpretation of Wetlands) is essential for this workshop. *Work related experiences can be substituted for the introductory workshop.
Affiliation:US Geological Survey
Location:National Wetland Research Center
Contact Info: Kristi Broussard at 337-266-8699 or kbroussard@usgs.gov

Workshop for Administrators and the GIS Community
When:November 19, 2004 8:30:00 AM - 3:30:00 PM
Description:The US Geological Survey Louisiana NSDI Partnership Office presents Louisiana Spatial Data Volunteer Pilot Program & Louisiana Spatial Data Information Center Browser Workshop. This one-day workshop will describe the unique concept of the USGS Louisiana Spatial Data Partnership Office (LaSDPO) Volunteer Pilot Program – the first in the Nation. The workshop and subsequent discussions will consist of, but not limited to: (1) types of data being harvested, their current condition & value to The National Map, Homeland Security, First Responders, and the Louisiana GIS Community as a whole, (2) status & achievements of the LaSDPO Volunteer Program, (3) benefits to Louisiana, The Nation, and the participants, and (4) how Federal, State, local governments, organizations, the private sector, and individuals may become involved. The workshop will be comprised of lecture/slide presentation, hands-on experience with the “Louisiana Spatial Data Information Center Browser”, and handout information. The workshop is ideally suited for agencies administrators and GIS experienced individuals that may recognize the merit of the LaSDPO Volunteer Program and be in a position to participate or have the authority to delegate participation.
Affiliation:USGS
Location:USGS, Abdalla Hall, 635 Cajundome Blvd. Lafayette, LA
Contact Info: Kristi Broussard at kbroussard@usgs.gov or 337-266-8699
