Content Description

Abstract: The shelf can be divided into two regions or regimes. The outer shelf controlled Iarqely by the Gulf Stream and associated Gulf Stream Western Boundary events, such as meanders and eddies having time scales of several days to several weeks, and the inter and mid-shelf where semidiurnal tide and direct wind forcing appear dominant. In the vicinity of Charleston, the relative importance of processes changes somewhat with the Gulf Stream apparently being deflected offshore due to interaction with a local bathemetric feature called the Charleston Bump. This general pattern described above is supported by the long-term mooring.
