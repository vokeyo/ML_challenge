A Day in the Life of the Sun City Gals

A Window Installation by Colleen Kinsella

June 16 - July 11, 2008

Gallery Hours: Wednesday - Friday, 12 - 6:00 PM, or by chance or appointment

SPACE is pleased to present A Day in the Life of the Sun City Gals, a window installation by Colleen Kinsella, made in conjunction with the The Brothers Unconnected, a tribute to the music of Sun City Girls and Charles Gocher, to be performed at SPACE on Friday, June 20.

Formed in 1981 by brothers Richard and Alan Bishop and percussionist Charlie Gocher, Sun City Girls rendered an eclectic mix of every musical style one can think of - demented surf, Indian and Asian improvisation, free-form noise, fractured folk, you name it - with doses of kabuki theater, shadow puppetry, and maverick guerilla stagecraft thrown in. Sadly, Charles Gocher passed away on February 19th, 2007 from a long battle with cancer. Brothers Unconnected will feature Charles' video experiments followed by Alan and Richard performing Sun City Girls songs as an acoustic guitar duet. This tour will most likely be the only time this show will be presented as such and is a tribute both to Charles Gocher and selected music from the 27-year legacy of Sun City Girls. Opening are Sun City Girls compa?eros, Portland's own beloved Fire On Fire, which Kinsella is part of.

Using paper as sculpture to create her view of Sun City Girls live, Kinsella brings color, dimension and life to Charles Gocher?s memorial in her window installation, an elaborately constructed, large scale, tunnel book. Keep your eyes open. The rest is a secret.

About the artist:

Colleen Kinsella is an artist and musician living in South Portland, Maine. She received a BFA in Photography and Printmaking from Syracuse University. Recent solo shows include: Colleen Kinsella, Washington Arts Association, Litchfield, CT, Blood Ties, Artist Image Resource Gallery, Pittsburgh, PA, and Turbulent Times, Cooper Jackson Gallery, Portland, ME. Kinsella was chosen for the 2005 Portland Museum of Art Biennial. She has shown her work in numerous group shows throughout Maine at CMCA, the Drawing Room, June Fitzpatrick, Haystack School of Arts and Crafts, among others.
