Pedestrian Dies from Injuries Sustained in 9/13/06 Truck / Pedestrian Crash
09/18/2006
Lieutenant Gregg Hastings
Public Information Officer
Office: (503) 731-3020 ext. 247

Oregon State Police have been informed that DOUGLAS HOWARD HOUSTON, age 43, from Glendale, died on September 15th from injuries suffered two days before when he was struck by a commercial truck and trapped underneath an axle until he could be extricated by fire department personnel.
 
On September 13, 2006 at approximately 4:30 a.m., a 1996 Freightliner truck towing a semi-trailer was southbound on Interstate 5 near milepost 286 in the far right lane. The driver, JUSTO C. CIRU, age 55, from Ryderwood, Washington, stated he was following another truck when it swerved to avoid something. CIRU then swerved and braked but was unable to avoid a pedestrian he said was in the right hand lane. The right front of the truck struck the pedestrian. The truck then came to a stop on the right southbound shoulder.
 
The pedestrian, DOUGLAS H. HOUSTON, age 43, from Glendale and Grants Pass areas, was trapped underneath the right side dual axle. Tualatin Valley Fire & Rescue personnel responded to the scene. Emergency personnel were able to extricate HOUSTON from where he was trapped. LifeFlight responded to the scene and transported to HOUSTON to Oregon Health Sciences University in critical condition where he was treated until he died September 15th.
 
Oregon State Police troopers are continue to investigate the crash. Any witnesses traveling in the area who observed a pedestrian near or in the freeway lanes is asked to contact OSP Northern Command Center dispatch at 800-452-7888.
 
No citations have been issued.
