                      Magnet     field     calculations

 

 

   In a sextupole, the desired magnetic field varies as r² across the aperture of the magnet on the median plane, r being the distance from the magnet center. A sextupole field can be generated by a magnet pole shape in which the pole surface is given by r³cos3θ = const, where  θ  is  the azimuthal angle measured from the pole center.  However, the  r³cos3θ pole has to end somewhere, to allow room for the exciting coils, and this limits the quality of the field achieved. The skew sextupole has a 6-fold symmetry and the centers of opposite poles laying in the median plane. In this case symmetry requires that the only multipoles present are b2, b8 and b14. It is possible to vanish multipole b8, if cut off the pole at the angle 18.6° and r1/ro = 1.212.

Fig. 1 shows the magnetic field quality for such pole profile. It is also possible to increase the good field quality ( 0.1% ) region up to r = 50 mm shimming the pole ends.

    It was used OPERA 2D code for the magnet field calculations with total number 10942 finite elements and 22255 nodes for the lattice.
