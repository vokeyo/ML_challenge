Attorney General's Statement Regarding The Filing Of A Settlement Between SCG, CNG And The State Office Of Consumer Counsel

October 25, 2001

The following is a statement from Attorney General Richard Blumenthal on today's filing of a settlement between the state Office of Consumer Counsel (OCC) and Southern Connecticut Gas Company and Connecticut Natural Gas. The settlement stems from several dockets currently pending before the Department of Public Utility Control (DPUC) to investigate overearnings by both companies, imprudence with respect to Southern's gas supply management, and certain questionable transactions with Southern's affiliates:

"This settlement is a disservice to Connecticut consumers. Only days away from Halloween, it provides treats for the companies and tricks for consumers. These companies overearned by more than $20 million in the past year, and they are likely to continue to overearn by at least that much in the coming years. Under this settlement, the companies will retain virtually all those overearnings, which should rightfully be returned to consumers in the form of substantial reductions in customer bills.

"The $2 million rate reduction proposed in this settlement is illusory -- an insult to hardworking consumers. This so-called rate reduction will vaporize as consumers are forced to pay for bad business decisions, exorbitant gas costs, and other expenses that should properly be paid by the company. In fact, the final effect of this settlement will likely be an increase in average consumer bills, even as the companies overearn by millions of dollars and gas prices decline across the country.

"In agreeing to this settlement the OCC has abandoned significant consumer protection litigation currently pending before our Connecticut courts and has agreed not to seek future rate reductions, while receiving nothing in return.

"My office is not bound by this agreement, and I will fight it, urging the DPUC to reject the settlement as an affront to Connecticut consumer protection law and a disservice to Connecticut ratepayers."
